Risotto alla Milanese

Risotto alla Milanese is a luxurious and creamy Italian rice dish, infused with saffron for a stunning golden color and a rich, buttery texture. It’s a dish born in Milan, often served alongside Osso Buco, but also perfect on its own as a comforting, elegant meal. Each spoonful offers warmth, aroma, and depth — a true symbol of northern Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

Arborio rice, saffron threads, butter, olive oil, onion, dry white wine, chicken or vegetable broth, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.

Instructions

  1. Warm the broth: In a pot, heat your chicken or vegetable broth and keep it warm over low heat.
  2. Sauté the onion: In a large pan, melt butter and add a drizzle of olive oil. Sauté finely chopped onion until soft and translucent.
  3. Toast the rice: Add the Arborio rice and stir for 2–3 minutes until it becomes slightly translucent at the edges.
  4. Deglaze with wine: Pour in the white wine and stir until it’s mostly absorbed.
  5. Add saffron: Dissolve the saffron threads in a small amount of warm broth, then pour into the rice for its signature golden color.
  6. Add broth gradually: Slowly add a ladle of warm broth at a time, stirring constantly. Let each addition absorb before adding the next.
  7. Finish with butter and cheese: Once creamy and al dente, remove from heat and stir in butter and Parmesan cheese until silky.
  8. Serve immediately: Garnish with extra cheese and a few saffron strands for a touch of elegance.
